How Pakistani Businesses Survivied Through Lockdown

  1. Home
  2. Blog
Aug 27 2020 | Data From Coronatracker.com

Pakistani Businesses in the past 4 months

Posted by Birbal Books

The lockdown in Pakistan was enforced on the 1st of April. Pakistani Businesses have been suffering since. While the MSME (Micro, Small, and Medium Enterprises) have been struggling to recover some sectors of the country prospered such as the IT sector which reported a growth of about 23.7% as compared to the last fiscal year. Pakistani exports of mango also saw a surge surpassing the target of 80,000 tons and reaching 125,000, the global increase in the price of cotton (3%), and the increase in demand of textile also helped Pakistani exports.

But even with the country doing so well with the IT sectors and exports the average Pakistani Businessman is suffering heavy losses on all sides, mainly because nearly none of them have had planned for a day so rainy. The lockdown imposed a heavy barrier in the transport of goods in Pakistan and disrupted the supply chain. The lack of demand for items such as mobile phones and electronic devices also caused heavy losses. Even the real state department saw a loss in value. And for a country who's MSME are crucial to the economy and are responsible for 40% of the GDP this was not good news. More can be found in this article.

Even with all these pebbles in the path, Pakistani Businesses found a way to stay afloat. From offices setting up "WORK FROM HOME" to Businesses quickly jumping on to home delivery methods. Regardless if they have their necessary resources for proper dealings at their homes Pakistani Businesses did their best to keep working. Pakistan saw a surge in remote work and home delivery and after a few months, the "SMART LOCKDOWN" was introduced which removed complete restrictions and provided flexible work hours.

Doing their best with whatever they had, Pakistani Businesses did their most diligent to keep the economy afloat. However the virus has not yet been defeated and a vaccine is still to be made. Just in the last 24 hours (24 Aug 2020) Pakistan saw nearly 500 cases, hence for the average Pakistani Business the event of a complete shutdown is still alive but now the knowledge of such an event to occur is present and the MSME can prepare themselves accordingly.
